Summaya Adam

Sr Summaya Adam was raised in New York City. She started her elementary education at PS69Q and she was awarded Valedictorian of her graduating class in 2001 from Bayside High School. As she continued on in her studies, she graduated with her Bachelors degree from New York University (NYU) with a minor in early childhood education and a Masters degree from Nova Southeastern University (NSU). During the course of her educational career, she achieved one-of-a-kind scholarships that included New York Newsday, New York Times, Gates Millenium Scholars, Coca Cola, Lewis Rudin Affiliates amongst others.

Her life's journey did not immediately lead her down the path to education. In 2007, as an entrepreneur she started her own IT consulting company where she worked with numerous Fortune 500 companies to present. Continuing with her successful entrepreneurship, Sr Summaya felt a strong desire to give back to the Muslim community by creating a motivating and nurturing environment to give rise to the next generation of Muslim leaders. She realized her passion through her interaction with special education children and hands on classroom experience. She holds a Georgia Teaching Certification in Early Childhood Education and taught first grade at Hamzah Academy.

She believes Islam is the greatest gift that Allah has given to the Muslim community through the Prophet Muhammad (SAW) hence, Sr Summaya believes seeking and delivering knowledge is a sacred duty Muslims can participate in. Her dedication to ensure that Islamic principles along with secular education are balanced together for young minds is clearly illustrated with the merger of Islamic themes in classroom subjects.

Sr Summaya believes school should be fun so that students are motivated to learn. At Hamzah Academy teachers are urged to plan engaging hands-on lessons and exciting supplemental activities to enrich the students' learning experience. Parents are an integral part of the learning process and are encouraged to get involved with their child's education. Sr Summaya is thankful to the Almighty for a chance to disseminate religious knowledge and values for the much-needed space for young Muslims.

Salvy Ahsan Muhammad

Sr Salvy Ahsan Muhammad is pleased to be returning to Hamzah Academy as the Islamic Studies teacher. She really enjoyed her time as the second grade teacher in the 2012-2013 school year, and is excited to be a part of this great school again.

Sr. Salvy graduated magna cum laude from Georgia State University with a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology. She is also a 2011 graduate of the Karamah Law and Leadership program for Muslim women in Washington, D.C. Furthermore, she has gained some academic and field experience in Mental Health Counseling. Currently, she is completing the diploma program in Islamic Studies on Islamic Online University. inshAllah she plans to complete her Master's degree in Islamic Studies on Islamic Online University as well.

Sr. Salvy has been a leader in several community initiatives, such as the Georgia Muslim Students Association, Atlanta Muslim Young Professionals, and Project Hope. In pursuing these efforts, she had the opportunity to promote the Muslim identity across metro Atlanta and educational opportunities for refugee women and children.

Ms. Salvy has always had the passion to work with children. She likes to attribute her devotion to becoming an educator to her own elementary school teachers. While in high school, she volunteered as an Assistant to the Director of a home day care. At the university level, she served as a writing tutor for Psychology students. Her post graduate endeavors include working as a Sunday school teacher at Islamic Society of South Atlanta, homeschooling administrator at Darul Uloom Academy, and substitute teacher at ILM Academy. Furthermore, she has worked with the Urban Child Study Center investigating the quality of language and literacy instruction and young children's early language and literacy skills in Metro Atlanta.

Sr. Salvy is committed to making Islamic education a primary priority. She understands the value of a comprehensive Islamic Studies program that reaches out to every child's unique strengths and learning styles. As the Islamic Studies teacher, Sr. Salvy will try to relate Islamic principles with her student's secular studies, personal, and social lives. In order for a child to reach his or her fullest potential, she feels that it is vital for there to be effective communication between the parents and her. Sr. Salvy hopes through her guidance and support from parents, she will be able to help her students gradually develop their concept of Iman and teach them Islamic ways of worship, Adab, and Akhlaq.
